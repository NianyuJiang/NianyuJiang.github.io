In Mass General Hospital, Wellman Center for Photomedicine, Dr. Evans' Lab, I am currently researching, designing and developing fluoroscence based optochemical sensors for transcutaneous pO2 and pCO2 measurement.  Under light with specific wavelength exitation, florophores under different gas concentration delivers varied emission which can be detected and quantified to associated with the gas partial pressure. The mission is to utilized the developed material for non-invasive pO2 and pCO2 measurement on patients. The sensor consisits three parts:
1. Fluoroscense material; these material usualy react with O2 and CO2 accompanied with excitation/emission alter.
2. Electronics: usually LEDs, photodiodes and microcontroller for performing light excitation, measuring emission and collecting signal.
3. Software: for data collection, calibration and interpretation.
