In Mass General Hospital, Wellman Center for Photomedicine, Dr. Rox Anderson's Lab, I have been developing a portable-level device which can potentially perform rapid diagnose and treatment for pneumothorax, the device consists of three parts:
1. A base block which is adhere to the patient, between 4th and 5th intercostal space at the anterior axillary line
2. A sliding block which contains ultrasound probe, locating intercostal spaces and confirm pneumothorax beneath
3. A ratatable arm which contains a pre-loaded pneumothorax catheter for rapid treatment

My Critial Role and Accomplishments:
---
1. Designed and developed the mechanical structural for all three blocks.
2. Designed and developed a central control unit, together with multiple sensors for the precise positioning and 
3. Designed a dual-hydralic locomotive system to advance the catheter while stop at pleural space.
4. Built and Trained a ML model to recognize the intercostal spaces and pneumothorax sites from the ultrasound image.
5. Performed an animal study to validate the devices' proof-of-concept with a surgical team.
