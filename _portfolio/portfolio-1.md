In IOWA state university, Drs. Shrotriya and Nilsen-Hamilton Labs, I have been woking on the research, design and development of electrochemical sensors for bio-particle detection. The sensor generally consists of four parts:
1. Substrates, usually conductive electrodes with high surface-to-volumn ratio
2. Bio-recognizer, usually aptamers, peptides, mRNAs, etc.
3. Bio-target, usually all kinds of small bioparticles such as proteins, oligonucleotides, drugs, etc.
4. Detector, Electrochemical based methods, such as electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS), cylic voltammetry (CV), etc.

My Critical Role and Accomplishment:
---
At Engineering side:
1. Explored and optimized the fabrication methods of substrates for the successful transducing binding signals between bio-target and bio-recognizer into detectable electrical signals.
2. Modeled the sensor system into comparable electrical circuits and co-related the binding induced signal with bio-target concentrations.
3. Miniaturized the potentiostat, reducing both the cost and size from Lab instrument to wearable size.
4. Developed software for reacting with sensors and plotting the target concentration at real-time

At Biochemisty & Molecular Biology side:
1. Modeled and configured the dynamic reaction between bio-target and bio-recognizer for the validation of induced signal change.
2. Explored and validated methods for optimized bio-recognizer surface functionalized.
