I myself is gradually becoming a heavy AI user starting at 2024, after ChatGPT has released. I used to learn AI by the earlist at 2018, when AI was mostly used to do image classification and speech detection, however, the giant leap both at hardware, software and methodology make the LLM type of AI tools born, significantly reshaped how I am doing the engineering work.

Without question, "using AI to find the correct answer" is not guaranteed. As a engineer R&D, the absolute correct answer itself may not exist, since the question I am exploring at has no confirm answer yet. Thinking on what AI can help me conclude the current solutions is actually becoming what I mostly relied on.

For example, you may ask AI:" what is the best option to monitor the arterial blood gas?" clearly you missed a lot information such as: what is the environment setup? what is the turnover time? invasive or non-invasive? to what accuracy do you need? etc. AI provide a lot of method which I do not know. To the most I know two methods, 1. using "Clark electrode" where O2 react with electrods and generate current, 2.  Optical fluorescence which is sensitive to O2. AI gave me other methods such as Paramagnetic oxygen sensing, Solid-state semiconductor oxygen sensors, Electron paramagnetic resonance, and Mass spectrometry. 

Thanks to AI, I now knew the most applied methods for ABG pO2 measuring, now time to ask AI to find the nice paper explaining the mechanism of these methods, the commercialized product which is using these methods, clinical trails which use these products, and review papers which compares these products. From here, I can get a better and broader vision of what people have tried and most importantly, **WHY** they tried these.

I keep using this way to evaluate the AI comtout myself. I find its horrible to let AI replace my thinking. As R&D, AI helps me greatly, much more easier to gain vision which I don't have, find and collect research that I neven know, but I always keep the core concept:
**Do not let AI replace your thinking**
